San Diego Chapter

The San Diego chapter is the southernmost node of PLRCAWG's statewide network — organizing in one of California's most significant agricultural border regions. San Diego County contains some of the state's highest-value specialty crop production alongside extreme patterns of land concentration, absentee ownership, and farmworker precarity.

The San Diego Buyers Club is currently forming — building the organized consumer base that will anchor the region's Territorial Market. This chapter is in early formation and actively looking for organizers, researchers, farmers, and community members to help build it from the ground up.

Building in This Chapter

San Diego Buyers Club

Forming now — building the organized consumer base to anchor the San Diego Territorial Market. We are looking for community groups, co-ops, mutual aid networks, and tenants organizations to join.

Land Acquisition Research

Mapping agricultural land patterns in San Diego County — identifying absentee-owned parcels and pathways to campesin@ collective tenure in the border region.

Engineering Brigade

No active project yet. We are looking for students from San Diego universities and technical workers interested in launching the first Brigade project in this region.

Political Education

Formation sessions connecting San Diego organizers to the statewide curriculum — including border agrarian movement history and Baja California campesino organizing.
