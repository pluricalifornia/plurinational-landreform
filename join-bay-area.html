Bay Area Chapter

The Bay Area chapter covers the Northern and Central Coast region — from the San Francisco Bay to the Salinas Valley corridor. This chapter is home to the founding Territorial Market collaboration: Stanford Housing Co-ops purchasing collectively from the Coalicion de Pequenos Agricultores. The Berkeley Student Housing Co-ops and Bay Area Buyers Club are in early formation, expanding the network.

On the engineering side, Tech for Liberation at Stanford is converting a trailer into mobile, solar-powered cold storage for the Coalicion — eliminating cooler rental fees and giving farmers collectively owned refrigeration infrastructure.

Active in This Chapter

Territorial Markets

Stanford Housing Co-ops buy collectively from the Coalicion. Bay Area Buyers Club and Berkeley Student Housing Co-ops are forming. Consumer and producer coordination is ongoing.

Engineering Brigade

Tech for Liberation (Stanford) is converting a trailer into mobile solar cold storage for the Coalicion — eliminating cooler rental fees and giving farmers owned cold infrastructure.

Land Acquisition

Regional mapping of agricultural land patterns, absentee ownership analysis, and identifying parcels suitable for campesin@ collective tenure in the Bay Area and Central Coast.

Political Education

Formation sessions rooted in agrarian political economy and the theory of change — connecting Bay Area organizers to the statewide network.
